## Campaign 1: The 30-Second Glow Finder

**Strategy brief:** Engage users with a fun, 3-question interactive DM quiz to identify their skin type and recommend the perfect serum/moisturizer duo. Once they get their personalized match, reveal the limited-time $40 threshold offer to secure the free travel cleanser.

**Conversational flow**

*   **Business:** Hey [Name]! ✨ Quick question for you... if you could solve just ONE skin frustration today, what would it be? (Tap one below to start our 30-second Glow Finder!)
    *   *[Button: Dryness/Flakiness]*
    *   *[Button: Dullness/Dark Spots]*
    *   *[Button: Fine Lines/Aging]*
*   *Branch 1: User taps [Dullness/Dark Spots]*
    *   **Business:** Ah, the quest for that lit-from-within glow! We get it. To give you the best match, is your skin typically sensitive or more on the oily/combination side?
        *   *[Button: Sensitive/Dry]*
        *   *[Button: Oily/Combo]*
    *   *Branch 1a: User taps [Oily/Combo]*
        *   **Business:** Perfect. Our Vitamin C Glow Serum is literally made for you—it brightens without feeling heavy. Pair it with our lightweight Hibiscus Water Cream ($42 total) and you'll hit our sweet spot. Plus, since it's over $40, I’ll automatically slide our Travel Cleanser ($15 value) into your cart for free today! 🎁 Want me to send the direct checkout link for this duo?
            *   *User (interested):* Yes, please! -> **Business:** Here you go! [Link] The free cleanser is already loaded in. Can't wait to hear how your skin loves it! 💛
            *   *User (not now):* I'll think about it. -> **Business:** No rush at all! Just a heads-up, the free cleanser promo ends Sunday. I'll leave the link here if you want to check it out later: [Link]. Have a beautiful day!

**A/B test variations**

*A/B test — Opening message*
- **Variation A:** "Hey [Name]! ✨ Quick question for you... if you could solve just ONE skin frustration today, what would it be?"
- **Variation B:** "Ready for your healthiest skin yet, [Name]? 🌿 Take our 30-second Glow Quiz to find your perfect natural serum match!"
- **Rationale:** Variation A uses a curiosity-gap question focusing on a pain point, which typically drives higher initial response rates (the "hook" lever) compared to Variation B, which reads more like a standard marketing pitch.

*A/B test — Key in-flow CTA*
- **Variation A:** "Want me to send the direct checkout link for this duo?"
- **Variation B:** "Tap here to claim your free cleanser and shop the duo: [Link]"
- **Rationale:** Variation A leverages permission-based marketing (the "friction reduction" lever). Asking for permission to send a link builds trust and keeps the conversation natural, whereas dropping a raw link too early (Variation B) can trigger sales resistance.

**Simulated KPI predictions** *(plausible planning estimates, not guarantees)*

| Metric | Estimate |
| --- | --- |
| Open rate | 75% |
| Click-through rate | 22% |
| Conversion rate | 6.2% |

**PM rationale:** 
The 75% open rate is anchored to typical Instagram DM broadcast and automation open rates, which dwarf standard email open rates (~20%) due to direct lock-screen push notifications. The 22% CTR is highly plausible because the interactive quiz format builds micro-commitments; users who answer two questions are highly primed to click their personalized recommendation. The 6.2% conversion rate is driven by the hyper-relevance of the product match and the natural cart-building effect of bundling a serum and moisturizer to cross the $40 threshold for the free gift.

## Campaign 2: The Insider's Upgrade

**Strategy brief:** Reward your most engaged Instagram followers with an exclusive "secret" upgrade. This direct-outreach campaign targets users who have recently interacted with your posts or stories, offering them VIP access to the free travel-size cleanser with any purchase.

**Conversational flow**

*   **Business:** Hey [Name]! Thanks so much for showing our recent posts some love. 💖 Because you're part of our close circle here, I wanted to let you in on a little secret before we post it on our main feed...
*   *Branch 1: User replies (interested)*
    *   **User:** Ooh, what's the secret?
    *   **Business:** This week, we're doing a secret "Travel Light" upgrade. If you grab any of our natural serums or moisturizers (orders $40+), we’re tucking our bestselling Travel Cleanser into your box for free. It’s perfect for keeping your skin fresh on the go! Want the secret link to claim yours?
        *   *User:* Yes, send it! -> **Business:** Here is your VIP link: [Link]. The free cleanser will automatically apply at checkout. Let me know if you need help picking the right serum for your skin!
*   *Branch 2: User asks a product question*
    *   **User:** Which serum is best for dry skin?
    *   **Business:** Oh, definitely our Avocado Nourishing Serum! It's super rich in fatty acids. Pair it with our Rosewater Moisturizer ($44 total) and your skin will stay hydrated all day. Plus, that gets you the free cleanser! Here's the link to both: [Link].

**A/B test variations**

*A/B test — Opening message*
- **Variation A:** "Hey [Name]! Thanks so much for showing our recent posts some love. 💖 Because you're part of our close circle here..."
- **Variation B:** "Hey [Name]! Quick heads-up: we have a free gift for you this week. 🎁"
- **Rationale:** Variation A leverages reciprocity and exclusivity (the "belonging" lever). By acknowledging their past engagement, it feels like a genuine relationship, whereas Variation B immediately feels transactional.

*A/B test — Key in-flow CTA*
- **Variation A:** "Want the secret link to claim yours?"
- **Variation B:** "Shop now to get your free cleanser: [Link]"
- **Rationale:** Variation A maintains the "insider" narrative (the "curiosity" lever). Asking if they want the link builds anticipation, while Variation B breaks the illusion of exclusivity by presenting a generic sales link too quickly.

**Simulated KPI predictions** *(plausible planning estimates, not guarantees)*

| Metric | Estimate |
| --- | --- |
| Open rate | 85% |
| Click-through rate | 15% |
| Conversion rate | 4.5% |

**PM rationale:**
Because this campaign targets warm leads (past engagers), the open rate is projected at an exceptional 85%. The CTR (15%) is lower than the quiz campaign because it is a more direct pitch without the interactive discovery element. However, the conversion rate remains strong at 4.5% because these users already have brand affinity and are highly motivated by exclusive, insider perks.

## Campaign 3: The TSA-Approved Glow Up

**Strategy brief:** Address the common pain point of maintaining a skincare routine while traveling. Position the free travel cleanser as the ultimate travel companion to their favorite full-size serum or moisturizer, making packing hassle-free.

**Conversational flow**

*   **Business:** Hey [Name]! ✈️ Got any fun trips planned for the summer, or are you keeping it local?
*   *Branch 1: User is traveling*
    *   **User:** Going to Europe next month!
    *   **Business:** Oh, how exciting! 🗺️ Travel skin is so real though—the cabin air always dries us out. To help you keep your glow on the go, we're giving away our Travel-Size Cleanser (TSA-approved!) for free with any order over $40 this week. It pairs perfectly with our Daily Dew Moisturizer ($42) to lock in hydration. Want me to drop the link so you can pack light?
        *   *User:* Yes please, that sounds perfect. -> **Business:** Here you go! [Link] Safe travels, and enjoy that gorgeous, hydrated vacation skin! 🌟
*   *Branch 2: User is staying local*
    *   **User:** No trips planned, just staying home!
    *   **Business:** Honestly, staycations are the best! 🏡 Even at home, having a mini cleanser is super handy for gym bags or overnight stays. Plus, it's a great way to try our gentle wash for free when you restock your favorite serum. Want to see our bestseller list? [Link]

**A/B test variations**

*A/B test — Opening message*
- **Variation A:** "Hey [Name]! ✈️ Got any fun trips planned for the summer, or are you keeping it local?"
- **Variation B:** "Hey [Name]! Don't let travel ruin your skin. Get our free TSA-approved cleanser this week!"
- **Rationale:** Variation A uses a lifestyle-focused question (the "relatability" lever) that starts a natural conversation. Variation B jumps straight into a problem/solution marketing message, which can feel intrusive in a private DM space.

*A/B test — Key in-flow CTA*
- **Variation A:** "Want me to drop the link so you can pack light?"
- **Variation B:** "Get your travel duo here: [Link]"
- **Rationale:** Variation A focuses on the ultimate benefit—packing light (the "utility" lever)—rather than the transaction of buying a "duo" (Variation B). 

**Simulated KPI predictions** *(plausible planning estimates, not guarantees)*

| Metric | Estimate |
| --- | --- |
| Open rate | 70% |
| Click-through rate | 18% |
| Conversion rate | 5.0% |

**PM rationale:**
The 70% open rate is solid for a lifestyle-led opener. The 18% CTR is driven by the seasonal relevance of travel; women in the 28-45 demographic are highly active travelers who value convenience and TSA-friendly skincare. The conversion rate (5.0%) is supported by the price-point alignment: since the Daily Dew Moisturizer is $42, purchasing just one core item immediately unlocks the free cleanser, creating a very low barrier to conversion.

## Recommended next step

Run **Campaign 1: The 30-Second Glow Finder** first. 

Skincare is highly personal, and consumers in the 28-45 demographic are often hesitant to buy products online without knowing if they suit their specific skin concerns. By starting with a consultative, interactive quiz, you remove the choice paralysis. This campaign naturally guides the customer to purchase a recommended duo (e.g., a serum and moisturizer), which seamlessly pushes their cart value over the $40 threshold to unlock the free cleanser. It offers the best combination of high engagement (CTR) and maximum average order value (AOV).
