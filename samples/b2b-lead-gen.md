## Campaign 1: The "Late Payment Leak" Calculator

**Strategy brief:** Engage freelancers by helping them calculate the hidden cost of manually chasing unpaid invoices. By answering three quick questions in Messenger, they receive a personalized "leak score" along with the free "Get Paid Faster" checklist and an invite to the 30-day trial.

**Conversational flow**

*   **Business:** Hi [First Name]! Quick question for you: How many hours did you spend chasing late payments from clients last month? 
    *   *Button A: 1-3 hours*
    *   *Button B: 4+ hours*
    *   *Button C: Luckily, none!*
*   **User clicks "1-3 hours" or "4+ hours":**
    *   **Business:** That adds up fast. Over a year, that’s up to 48 hours of unbillable admin work—essentially a full week of unpaid labor. We built a free "Get Paid Faster" checklist to help you automate this and reclaim that time. Would you like me to send the PDF right here?
    *   *Button: Yes, send the checklist!*
*   **User clicks "Luckily, none!":**
    *   **Business:** Love to hear that! You're ahead of the curve. If you ever want to keep it that way as you scale, we have a "Get Paid Faster" checklist with system setups that keep client accounts current automatically. Want a copy just in case?
    *   *Button: Sure, send it over.*
*   **User clicks "Yes, send the checklist!" or "Sure, send it over":**
    *   **Business:** Here is your download link: [Checklist Link]. 
    *   **Business:** To help you put this on autopilot today, we’re also offering freelancers 30 days of our automated invoicing tool completely free. No credit card required. Would you like to set up your free trial account now?
    *   *Button: Start Free 30-Day Trial*
    *   *Button: Tell me how it works first*
*   *(Branch: User clicks "Tell me how it works first")*
    *   **Business:** It’s simple: you sync your clients, we send the invoices, and our system automatically sends polite, professional reminders if they miss the due date. Most freelancers get paid 3x faster. Ready to try it out for 30 days?
    *   *Button: Start Free 30-Day Trial*

**A/B test variations**

*A/B test — Opening message*
- **Variation A:** Hi [First Name]! Quick question for you: How many hours did you spend chasing late payments from clients last month?
- **Variation B:** Hey [First Name], did you know the average freelancer loses $5,000 a year to late payments and unbilled follow-up hours? How many unpaid invoices are you sitting on right now?
- **Rationale:** Testing *relatable time loss* (Variation A) vs. *financial loss/loss aversion* (Variation B). While financial loss is a strong motivator, freelancers are highly protective of their time, and Variation A lowers the friction to reply by asking a simple time-based multiple-choice question rather than asking them to admit to unpaid invoices.

*A/B test — Key in-flow CTA*
- **Variation A:** Start Free 30-Day Trial
- **Variation B:** Try Automating for Free (30 Days)
- **Rationale:** Testing *industry-standard terminology* (Variation A) vs. *outcome-oriented benefit language* (Variation B). Variation B reduces psychological friction by focusing on the action ("Automating") and the lack of cost ("Free") rather than the commitment of starting a "trial."

**Simulated KPI predictions**

| Metric | Estimate |
| --- | --- |
| Open rate | 78% |
| Click-through rate (Engagement) | 34% |
| Conversion rate (Lead Gen / Trial Sign-up) | 8.5% |

**PM rationale:** Messenger broadcasts and Click-to-Messenger ads yield significantly higher open rates (~70-80%) than traditional email (~20%) because they land in a personal chat app. The engagement rate is estimated at 34% because the opening question is low-friction and highly relatable to a freelancer's daily pain points. The final conversion to trial is projected at 8.5% because we deliver value first (the checklist) before asking for the trial sign-up, building trust within the conversation.

## Campaign 2: The "Get Paid Faster" Delivery Express

**Strategy brief:** A direct, high-value lead generation play that delivers the "Get Paid Faster" checklist instantly inside Messenger. Once the user secures their free resource, the assistant transitions them into starting their free 30-day trial to implement the checklist's steps automatically.

**Conversational flow**

*   **Business:** Hi [First Name]! Thanks for your interest in our "Get Paid Faster" checklist. It contains the exact 5 email templates and schedule we use to get invoices settled in under 48 hours. Tap below to download the PDF instantly!
    *   *Button: Download Checklist (PDF)*
*   **User clicks "Download Checklist (PDF)":**
    *   **Business:** Sent! You can access it here: [Checklist Link]. 
    *   **Business:** Quick question: Template #3 in that guide is an automated 3-day reminder. Would you prefer to copy-paste and send these follow-ups manually, or would you rather have a system send them for you automatically?
    *   *Button: I'll do it manually*
    *   *Button: Automate it for me*
*   *(Branch: User clicks "Automate it for me")*
    *   **Business:** Smart move. Your time is worth more than manual admin. You can try our automated reminder system free for 30 days. We'll set up your templates for you. Sound good?
    *   *Button: Yes, start my free trial*
*   *(Branch: User clicks "I'll do it manually")*
    *   **Business:** Totally fair! Doing it manually is a great way to start. If you ever find yourself spending too much time on it, remember you can try our automated tool free for 30 days. Save this link just in case you want to try it later: [Trial Link]. Good luck with the templates!

**A/B test variations**

*A/B test — Opening message*
- **Variation A:** Hi [First Name]! Thanks for your interest in our "Get Paid Faster" checklist. It contains the exact 5 email templates and schedule we use to get invoices settled in under 48 hours. Tap below to download the PDF instantly!
- **Variation B:** Hi [First Name]! Ready to stop chasing clients? Tap below to download your free "Get Paid Faster" checklist and get your unpaid invoices cleared this week.
- **Rationale:** Testing *specific asset utility* (Variation A, highlighting the "5 email templates") vs. *aspirational outcome* (Variation B, "stop chasing clients"). Specificity usually beats vagueness in B2B copy, so Variation A is expected to drive higher immediate downloads.

*A/B test — Key in-flow CTA*
- **Variation A:** Automate it for me
- **Variation B:** Tell me about the tool
- **Rationale:** Testing *high-intent action* (Variation A) vs. *low-friction information gathering* (Variation B). Variation B will likely get more clicks, but Variation A will yield higher-quality leads who are ready to start a trial immediately.

**Simulated KPI predictions**

| Metric | Estimate |
| --- | --- |
| Open rate | 82% |
| Click-through rate (Engagement) | 48% |
| Conversion rate (Lead Gen / Trial Sign-up) | 6.0% |

**PM rationale:** Because this campaign is triggered by users who actively opted in to receive the checklist (e.g., via a Facebook post or Click-to-Messenger ad), the open and initial engagement rates will be exceptionally high (82% and 48% respectively). The conversion to the 30-day trial is estimated at a conservative 6.0% because many users will simply grab the free resource and leave, but the high volume of initial engagements will still generate a substantial absolute number of trial sign-ups.

## Campaign 3: The "Awkward Conversation" Solver

**Strategy brief:** Tap into the emotional friction of freelancing—the awkwardness of asking clients for money. This campaign positions automation not just as a time-saver, but as a "neutral third party" that preserves client relationships, offering the trial as the ultimate solution.

**Conversational flow**

*   **Business:** Hi [First Name]. Let’s be honest: sending "friendly reminders" to clients about overdue invoices is awkward. Nobody likes playing the role of the debt collector. How do you usually handle overdue payments?
    *   *Button: I send manual emails*
    *   *Button: I call them*
    *   *Button: I just wait it out*
*   **User clicks "I send manual emails" or "I just wait it out":**
    *   **Business:** It really drains your creative energy, doesn't it? Our freelancers tell us that blaming "the system" is the easiest way to get paid without hurting client relationships. 
    *   **Business:** When an automated system sends the reminder, it's just "the software doing its job," not you being pushy. We have a free "Get Paid Faster" checklist that shows you how to set this up. Want the PDF?
    *   *Button: Yes, send the checklist*
*   **User clicks "Yes, send the checklist":**
    *   **Business:** Here you go: [Checklist Link]. 
    *   **Business:** If you want to completely remove yourself from the awkward follow-up process starting today, you can use our platform free for 30 days. We’ll handle the reminders, you keep the client relationships perfect. Win-win. Ready to try?
    *   *Button: Start Free 30-Day Trial*
    *   *Button: Maybe later*

**A/B test variations**

*A/B test — Opening message*
- **Variation A:** Hi [First Name]. Let’s be honest: sending "friendly reminders" to clients about overdue invoices is awkward. Nobody likes playing the role of the debt collector. How do you usually handle overdue payments?
- **Variation B:** Hi [First Name]. Want to preserve your client relationships? Let our automated system send your payment reminders so you don't have to play the bad guy. How do you currently follow up on late invoices?
- **Rationale:** Testing *empathy/problem alignment* (Variation A) vs. *solution presentation* (Variation B). Variation A is highly relatable and validating, which typically drives higher conversational engagement from freelancers who feel understood.

*A/B test — Key in-flow CTA*
- **Variation A:** Start Free 30-Day Trial
- **Variation B:** Try "Bad Cop" Automation Free
- **Rationale:** Testing *neutral SaaS language* (Variation A) vs. *playful, concept-aligned language* (Variation B). Variation B plays into the "neutral third party/bad cop" concept introduced in the conversation, which can increase CTR by keeping the tone consistent and lighthearted.

**Simulated KPI predictions**

| Metric | Estimate |
| --- | --- |
| Open rate | 75% |
| Click-through rate (Engagement) | 28% |
| Conversion rate (Lead Gen / Trial Sign-up) | 9.5% |

**PM rationale:** While the overall engagement rate (28%) may be slightly lower than the direct checklist delivery, the conversion rate to trial is projected to be the highest of the three campaigns at 9.5%. This is because the campaign addresses a deep psychological pain point (awkwardness and relationship anxiety) rather than just a logistics problem. When users realize that software can solve an emotional burden, their intent to try the product increases significantly.

## Recommended next step

**Run Campaign 2: The "Get Paid Faster" Delivery Express first.** 

**Why:** As an early-stage B2B SaaS startup, your primary goal is to build a database of leads (freelancers) and get immediate feedback on your checklist value. Campaign 2 has the lowest barrier to entry and the highest projected engagement rate (48%) because it directly fulfills the user’s immediate desire for the free resource. 

Starting here allows you to quickly build a custom audience in Meta, deliver immediate value, and establish a baseline conversion rate for your 30-day trial. Once you have steady traffic running through this direct funnel, you can introduce Campaign 3 as a retargeting flow for users who downloaded the checklist but didn't sign up for the trial, using the "awkward conversation" angle to overcome their final objections.
